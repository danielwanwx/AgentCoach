Super glad the book has been helpful!

Lots of nuance here, but we cover a lot of it in the Select: Choosing Responses.

It's common to reuse stories for different questions.
But it's often better to tell an additional story, since that gives you more opportunity to present aspects of your career that highlight more repeatable behaviorals, giving the interviewer confidence in hiring you.

Let's say the interview progressed like this:

Tell me about your favorite project.
...
Tell me about an ambiguous project.

If you didn't cover the ambiguity parts in detail for response #1, the parts where you broke down the problem, gathered additional data, etc., then I would tell those parts when responding to #3. But if you have already told that, I would mention the response to #1 just to remind the interviewer, and then try to tell another example.
